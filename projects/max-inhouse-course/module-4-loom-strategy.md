# Module 4: Loom Strategy

> "I kind of sped through this. I got to go to dinner. I hope you enjoy it. If you want, we can hop on a quick chat later."

The Loom bridges the gap between a DM conversation and booking a call. It delivers free value that positions you as the authority and creates reciprocity. This is where you prove you know what you're talking about without giving everything away.

---

## The Evergreen Loom Trick

This is how you send "custom" Looms without re-recording every time:

1. Record one evergreen Loom covering common pain points
2. Hit "Duplicate" before sending -- the duplicated Loom shows "made a couple minutes ago"
3. Rename to "Loom for [Name]" -- recipient feels it's custom-made for them
4. Feels personalized without re-recording every single time

---

## Loom Specifications

- **Length:** 60-120 seconds max
- **Feel:** Custom (or at least feel custom)
- **Content:** Show a specific result or tangible system
- **Visuals:** Show actual proof -- a business concept, Notion dashboard, visual walkthrough
- **Ending:** Clear but casual call to action

---

## Transition Scripts (DM to Loom)

### Script 1: Complexity Bridge

"Bro, just not gonna explain this over the DMs. It'd be too much back and forth, but I have something that directly solves scaling from 20 to 50. I can send you a quick walkthrough if you want it."

### Script 2: Empathy Bridge

"I used to struggle with the same thing, bro. Made a couple changes. If you want, I can shoot you over a Loom."

### Script 3: Effort Bridge

"You're making this way harder than it needs to be. Let me show you something real quick."

---

## Why People Say Yes to Looms

- They believe you're an authority who can teach them something
- You're offering free value with no strings attached
- The conversation has already positioned you as credible

## Why People Say No to Looms

- They don't think you're an authority
- They don't trust you yet
- They think it's irrelevant to their situation
- **Fix:** Better identity/profile work, better pain point discovery

---

## Loom Content Rules

- Don't do a full training or give away everything
- Show enough to demonstrate competence
- Leave them wanting more
- Show a tangible system or specific result
- Don't pitch. Just deliver value.

---

## Loom Ending (CTA)

This is the key. How you end the Loom determines whether they book.

**Don't say:** "Book a call with me"

**Do say:** "I kind of sped through this. I got to go to dinner. I hope you enjoy it. If you want, we can hop on a quick chat later."

Casual. Low-pressure. Positions the next step as natural, not a sales move.

---

## Action Items

1. Record one evergreen Loom (60-120 seconds) that covers the most common pain point your ICP faces. Show a tangible system or result.
2. Practice the duplicate-and-rename trick. Send your next 5 Looms using this method.
3. Memorize the three transition scripts (complexity bridge, empathy bridge, effort bridge) so you can use whichever fits the conversation.
4. Nail the casual ending. Practice saying the CTA until it sounds natural, not rehearsed.
5. Track your Loom acceptance rate. Target: 30-40% of conversations accept the Loom.
6. If acceptance is low, go back to Module 1 (identity) and Module 3 (pain point discovery). The Loom isn't the problem -- the setup is.

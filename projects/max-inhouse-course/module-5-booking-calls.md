# Module 5: Booking Calls

> Never say "call." The word "call" triggers sales guard. People associate it with being sold to and spending money.

This module covers how to transition from Loom to booked call, handle every objection, and maximize show-up rates.

---

## Core Rule: Never Say "Call"

The word "call" triggers sales guard. People associate it with being sold to and spending money. Replace with substitute words that frame free value instead.

---

## Reframing Templates

### Instead of "Let's hop on a call"

"Yo, I can share a screen with you tomorrow and walk you through."

### Instead of "Book a consultation"

"Look bro, I've got 15 minutes between meetings tomorrow if you want me to hop on a quick chat and show you how this works."

### Instead of "Schedule a strategy session"

"Yo, it'll be easier to just show you instead of spamming you in the DMs. Let me know when you got time to chat and we'll hop on."

### Instead of "Let's discuss your business"

"I can give you a breakdown of how we're doing this super quick. Just let me know when."

---

## Key Framing Elements

Each of these does specific psychological work:

- **"Between calls/meetings"** -- implies you're busy and in demand
- **"Quick chat"** -- low time commitment, not intimidating
- **"Show you"** -- giving value, not selling
- **"Super quick"** -- reduces perceived risk of wasting time
- **"Let me know when"** -- puts them in control, reduces pressure

---

## Handling Booking Objections

### "Is this a sales call?"

"No bro. I'm not going to pitch you or anything. Just figured I'd show you. I already cracked this part. 10 minutes max."

Then ACTUALLY hold to 10 minutes. Make them want to talk longer by being genuinely valuable.

### "Send me more info first"

"Bro, I just sent a Loom. If you want to hop on and get to know each other, let's hop on a chat. Let's do it."

A quick screen share is faster and clearer than text.

### "I'm too busy right now"

"No problem bro. Everyone's busy. That's why I keep these so short. I don't want to waste my time either. When things slow down a bit, let me know."

Detachment is key. "I'm too busy" is usually a redirect, not the truth. When you detach, they often come back: "Oh, maybe I will just hop on since he doesn't care."

### "Let me think about it"

"Yeah, bro. Take your time. Just so you know, I have like 15 minutes tomorrow. It's my only day I'm available. Super quick. Let me know if that works."

Create urgency through scarcity (limited availability). Then detachment.

## Show-Up Rate Optimization

Target: 80%+ show-up rate. Max hits ~92% due to authority + nurturing.

### Step 1: Send Booking Link After They Want It

- Don't send prematurely
- Wait until they've agreed to the concept

### Step 2: Get Confirmation (Via Voice Message)

- Don't text "did you book in?" -- it's annoying
- Send voice message: "Yo bro, just sent you the link. Let me know when you booked it. Looking forward to speaking with you."
- Voice = genuine. Text = nagging.

### Step 3: Day-Before Reminder

"Yo bro, you still good for tomorrow?"

Simple, direct, expected.

### Step 4: Day-Of Reminder

"Quick heads up bro, got you set for [time]. Send you a link 5 minutes before. Should be in your email."

Max's setter does this automatically as part of the process.

---

## Calendar Strategy

- Only show the next 3 days of availability
- Limited open spots = higher perceived value
- Makes them pick a time that works for YOU
- Subconsciously signals you're in demand

---

## Action Items

1. Purge the word "call" from your vocabulary. Practice the reframing templates until they're automatic.
2. Write out your versions of the 4 objection responses ("is this a sales call," "send more info," "too busy," "let me think about it"). Practice them.
3. Set up your booking link to only show the next 3 days of availability with limited slots.
4. Build the 4-step show-up sequence: booking link after agreement, voice message confirmation, day-before reminder, day-of reminder.
5. Track your booking rate (target: 50-60% of Loom viewers book) and show-up rate (target: 80%+).
6. If show-up rate is low, check: are you sending voice message confirmations? Day-before and day-of reminders?

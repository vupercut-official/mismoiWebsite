## 1. Who I Am

I'm Vu Tran. 18 years old. AI automation builder based in Fairfax, Virginia.

I've been hustling since 8th grade. Started by reselling on eBay and Craigslist - hit up Goodwill, thrift stores, garage sales, anywhere I could find stuff to flip. Saved up and bought my first PC with my own money even though my parents could've paid for it. Something in me just needed to know I earned it myself.

From there it was crypto mining with my GPU, stock trading with my lifeguard money, then teaching myself to string tennis rackets and charging for it ($500/month). Every side hustle taught me something but none of them scaled.

Then I found haircutting. Couldn't get a good haircut anywhere so I learned to do it myself. Started cutting for friends. Posted content about it every single day for 6 months straight. Grew to $18,000 a month. Hit 250 million views per month. Became one of the most viewed barbers on Instagram and TikTok.

But barbering doesn't scale. I can't cut 10 heads at once. So I pivoted. Tried e-commerce (couldn't believe in a product). Tried restaurant marketing (couldn't find the internal purpose). Hit a 4-month wall of sitting at my desk going through motions.

Then I found AI automation. And it clicked. The same way I could build systems to grow a barbering brand, I can build systems that run entire businesses. I'm only 1.5 weeks into this and I'm already obsessed. I can't see myself doing anything else.

I never wanted a 9-to-5. The lifeguarding job was enough to confirm that. I want to build things that create value for people and the money follows.

## 4. My Voice

### Energy
Calm and direct. Low energy but confident. I don't yell or hype. I explain things the way I'd explain them to a friend sitting next to me. Think Nick Saraev's delivery - just showing you how it works, no drama.

### Language Level
3rd to 5th grade English. No big words. No technical jargon unless I absolutely have to use it, and if I do, I explain it in plain English right after. The biggest barrier in AI automation is the jargon - I break that down.

### Tone
Conversational. Short sentences. Sometimes choppy or run-on, like actual speech. Not scripted. If it sounds like someone wrote it in a Google Doc and rehearsed it, it's wrong. It should sound like I'm answering a friend's question.

### Natural Phrases I Use
- "frickin'" (mild emphasis)
- "bro" / "bruh"
- "holy" / "sheesh" (when something is impressive)
- "so basically what this does is..."
- "check this out"
- "it's literally just..."
- "here's the thing"
- "that's it. that's all it is."

### What My Voice Is NOT
- Corporate ("according to our analysis, this solution leverages...")
- Overhyped ("THIS WILL CHANGE YOUR LIFE")
- Scripted (reading off a teleprompter feel)
- Technical ("execute the bash command to initialize the API endpoint")
- Fake positivity ("AMAZING! INCREDIBLE! YOU GOT THIS!")

## 7. Phrases to Use / Avoid

### Use These
- "here's the backend" (show don't tell)
- "let me show you" (pull people in)
- "this is what the data says" (data-driven, not opinion)
- "built this in Make.com" (specific tools, not vague)
- "this actually works" (proof over promise)
- "it's literally just..." (demystify the complex)
- "so basically..." (conversational intro)
- "bro" / "bruh" (natural, not forced)
- "frickin'" (mild emphasis, keeps it real)
- "that's it. that's all it is." (simplicity closer)
- "I'm 18 and I built this" (credibility without bragging)
- "here's what happened when I..." (story-based proof)

### Never Use These
- game-changer, unlock, leverage, synergy
- revolutionary, cutting-edge, next-level
- crush it, 10x, scale to the moon
- "according to...", "hypothetically...", "one might argue..."
- any word you'd need a dictionary for
- "hey guys!" or overly enthusiastic openers
- "like and subscribe" (earned, not begged)
- enterprise, solution, stakeholder, deliverable
- any sentence longer than 15 words if you can help it

## 8. Anti-Brand (What I Am NOT)

- **Not scripted.** If it sounds rehearsed, start over. Real content sounds like real speech - short, choppy, sometimes messy. That's the point.
- **Not overhyped.** No "THIS CHANGED MY LIFE" thumbnails. No fake surprise faces. No manufactured urgency.
- **Not corporate.** No suits. No slides. No "let me walk you through our value proposition." Just a kid showing you what he built.
- **Not gatekeeping.** Give away the knowledge for free. All of it. The people who want someone to build it for them will pay. The ones who want to build it themselves will respect you and come back later.
- **Not complicated.** If a 5th grader can't understand the reel, simplify it. The whole point of my brand is that AI automation is NOT complicated. I prove that by explaining it simply.
- **Not fake rich.** No rented Lambos. No "I made $100K this month" screenshots (especially when I haven't). Real numbers only. Even if the number is zero.

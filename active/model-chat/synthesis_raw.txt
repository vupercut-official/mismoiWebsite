## Synthesis: 6 Lead Magnet Offers for Social Media Agency Cold Email

---

### Consensus

Five points that all agents converged on, in order of strength:

1. **Value delivery in the email.** No "let me know if you're interested." The offer IS the email. Prompt, list, or audit included in the body.
2. **Open with their operations, not Vu's identity.** First line = observable fact from 90 seconds of LinkedIn research.
3. **Specificity is credibility.** No case studies means the proof is in the prompt quality and the precision of the observation. "Here's what I use" signals practitioner. "Here's a free template" signals vendor.
4. **CTA must require operational knowledge to answer.** "Which client would this break on?" is a better hook than "let me know if this helps" because only a real practitioner can answer it. Filters for engaged prospects automatically.
5. **The ladder rung was missing.** Free email to paid invoice is too big a jump. The async Loom audit fills the gap.

---

### Key Disagreements

**1. How many offers to ship simultaneously**

- Contrarian: Ship all 6 for breadth. More data faster.
- Pragmatist: Ship 3. Fewer variables, cleaner signal, statistical significance at lower volume.
- Edge-case-finder: Ship 3, rotate on 4-week cycles.

**Assessment: Pragmatist wins.** At zero revenue with no list infrastructure, running 6 concurrent offers creates attribution noise. Three offers at 50 sends each gives you enough signal to kill losers before wasting time on Offer 4-6. Ship 3, rotate.

---

**2. Offers 4-6: task-focused vs. outcome-focused**

- Task-focused camp (hashtag research, caption variants): Tangible, fast to understand, low resistance.
- Outcome-focused camp (retention signals, scope creep, competitive intel): Higher-value pain, closer to revenue, harder to commoditize.

**Assessment: Outcome-focused wins.** Agencies that manage 5-50 clients aren't fighting for 30 minutes on hashtags. They're fighting to retain clients, control scope, and justify retainers. Outcome-focused offers signal systems thinking, not task automation. Higher-value framing from the first email.

---

**3. Offer 6 (Loom audit): cold-send vs. warm-reply only**

- Pragmatist/user-advocate: Cold-send it. It's high perceived value, differentiates immediately.
- Edge-case-finder/contrarian: Cold Loom audits are unscalable at volume and create expectation of free work. Reserve for warm replies only.

**Assessment: Warm-reply only.** Sending a 20-minute personalized Loom cold, at scale, is not a lead magnet. It's a consulting offer you can't deliver at volume. The right move: use it as the upgrade offer when someone replies to Offers 1-5 but doesn't book. It becomes the missing ladder rung between engaged reply and paid invoice.

---

### Surprising Insights

- **The hashtag prompt is 2021.** Agents flagged this clearly. Social media agencies have already automated or deprioritized hashtag research. Leading with it signals you don't know the current state of the industry.
- **The CTA is a qualification mechanism.** "Which client would this break on?" doesn't just invite reply. It filters for people who are actually using a system. If they can answer it, they're a real prospect. If they can't, you learned something faster than a discovery call would tell you.
- **PAS-on-PAS-experts fails.** Social media agencies write copy professionally. A textbook Problem-Agitate-Solution structure reads as someone who learned cold email from a YouTube course. The winning frame is peer observation, not a framework. It should feel like a colleague noticed something about their setup.
- **"Systems analyst who studied agencies" is the right credential frame.** Not "I'm a practitioner like you" (can't claim it). Not "I'm a vendor with a solution" (commoditizes immediately). The frame is: "I've mapped how agencies at your scale operate and I see a specific gap in your setup."

---

### Final Recommendation

Ship Offers 1, 2, 3 in Wave 1. Test 4 and 5 in Wave 2. Keep Offer 6 as the warm-reply upgrade only.

## The 6 Offers

---

### Offer 1: The On-Brand Caption Engine

**What they get:** A Claude/ChatGPT prompt included in the email that generates on-brand captions from a 3-line brief. Drops into their workflow in 90 seconds.

**Why valuable:** Caption writing at scale is either templated or a bottleneck. This collapses 30-60 min per client per week into a system they can run today.

**CTA:** "Which client would this break on?"

**Bridge to paid:** Full content system build ($500-2K) -- caption engine, approval workflow, scheduling integration.

**Vu's time to deliver:** 5 min to send. Zero delivery time. It's in the email.

---

**Example email (87 words):**

> [Agency name] manages [X] accounts. At that scale, caption writing is either a system or someone's entire afternoon.
>
> Prompt I use to generate on-brand captions from a brief:
>
> *"You are a social media copywriter for [Brand]. Voice: [3 adjectives]. Platform: [platform]. Hook formula: [formula]. Input: [campaign brief]. Generate 3 caption variations, each under 150 characters."*
>
> Swap in your brand details and run it on your next post. Takes 90 seconds.
>
> Which client would this break on?
>
> -- Vu

### Offer 2: The Metrics-to-Narrative Translator

**What they get:** A prompt that converts raw performance data into a client-ready monthly report narrative. Included in the email, runs immediately.

**Why valuable:** Monthly reporting is a recurring 2-hour task per client. This compresses it to 15 minutes. The bottleneck isn't the data -- it's translating numbers into a story clients won't argue with.

**CTA:** "What metric do your clients push back on most?"

**Bridge to paid:** Full reporting automation (data pull, narrative generation, PDF formatting, auto-send) -- $1K-3K build.

**Vu's time to deliver:** 5 min to send. Zero delivery time.

---

> Prompt I use to turn raw metrics into client-ready copy:

**Vu's time to deliver:** 5 min to send. Zero delivery time.

### Offer 4: The Churn Signal Detector

**What they get:** A prompt that scans recent client communications for early churn signals -- declining response time, shorter replies, budget questions, reduced requests. Included in the email.

**Why valuable:** Most agencies find out a client is leaving 2 weeks before they cancel. This prompt runs in 5 minutes and surfaces the signals that are already in their inbox. High perceived value because it's tied directly to retained revenue.

**CTA:** "Which client are you least confident about right now? Run it there first."

**Bridge to paid:** Churn prevention system -- automated signal monitoring, alert triggers, response templates ($1.5K-4K build).

**Vu's time to deliver:** 5 min to send. Zero delivery time.

### Offer 5: The Scope Creep Ledger

**What they get:** A prompt that extracts undocumented scope additions from a client thread and outputs them as a table -- task, estimated hours, billed or not. Included in the email.

**Why valuable:** Scope creep is silent revenue leakage. Most agencies feel it but never quantify it. Running this prompt on one client thread surfaces hours of unbilled work that's already happened. High emotional resonance because it converts a vague frustration into a specific number.

**CTA:** "How much unbilled work do you find?"

**Bridge to paid:** Scope tracking and alert system ($1K-2K build) that flags new requests in real time and generates change order drafts automatically.

**Vu's time to deliver:** 5 min to send. Zero delivery time.

**What they get:** A 15-20 minute Loom walkthrough of one specific workflow they describe, with exact automation opportunities called out, built on Make.com. Personalized to their setup.

**Why valuable:** Everything before this is generic-enough to apply to most agencies. This is fully specific to their operation. It's the bridge between "interesting email" and "I want to hire this person." It requires investment from them (describing their workflow) which filters for serious prospects.

**CTA:** "Walk me through how you handle [workflow they mentioned]. I'll record what I see and send it back within 48 hours."

**Bridge to paid:** Direct conversation about scoping the build. The Loom IS the proposal.

**Vu's time to deliver:** 30-45 minutes per audit (research + record). Do not send cold. Reserve for warm replies only.

## Deployment Plan

**Wave 1 -- Weeks 1-3**

Ship Offers 1, 2, and 3 simultaneously. 50 sends each. Total: 150 sends.

Vary only the first line per send (90 seconds of research per prospect). Everything else stays fixed. This isolates whether the offer or the observation is driving replies.

**Kill signals (pull the offer at 50 sends if):**
- Reply rate under 3%
- Replies are "not interested" or "unsubscribe" with no operational content
- Nobody answers the CTA question with real operational detail

**Keep signals:**
- Reply rate above 5%
- Replies include specific client names, workflow descriptions, or genuine answers to the CTA
- At least 1 in 50 asks "how would you build this?"

---

**Wave 2 -- Weeks 4-6**

Kill the lowest performer from Wave 1. Replace with Offer 4 (Churn Signal). Run 50 more sends on the 2 surviving Wave 1 offers + Offer 4.

If Offer 4 outperforms either Wave 1 survivor at 50 sends, swap in Offer 5 (Scope Creep) and retire the next lowest performer.

**Wave 2 test variable:** Try two different CTA phrasings per offer. "Which client would this break on?" vs. "What would this change about your current process?" Track which generates longer replies.

---

**Offer 6 activation rule**

Do not cold-send. Activate only when:
- Prospect replied to an offer with real operational detail, AND
- Did not book a call within 5 days of reply

Then deploy the warm Loom offer. This is your conversion mechanism, not your lead generation mechanism.

---

**Sequencing summary**

| Week | Active Offers | Sends | Goal |
|------|--------------|-------|------|
| 1-3 | 1, 2, 3 | 50 each | Find 1-2 winners |
| 4-6 | Top 2 from W1 + Offer 4 | 50 each | Confirm winner, test Offer 4 |
| 7-9 | Top 2 + Offer 5 | 50 each | Full rotation established |
| Ongoing | 3 active offers | Rotate on 4-week cycles | Offer 6 triggers from warm replies |

Target: 1 paid conversation from every 150 sends by Week 6. If you hit that, the system is working. If not, the problem is the list quality or the first-line observation, not the offer.

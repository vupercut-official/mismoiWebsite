## Consensus

1. **Gate before everything.** Hard prerequisite gate (offer clarity + ICP definition) before any instruction. No offer, no reps. Pass/fail filter, not a module.

2. **Log from rep one.** Structured logging during outreach is the connective tissue. The log generates data, enables self-diagnosis, and feeds the KPI dashboard. Teaching without logged data first is teaching into a vacuum.

3. **Diagnostic over sequential.** Route by failure mode: nobody replies, replies but no calls, calls but no closes, closes but delivery breaks. Course teaches the fix after students hit the problem.

4. **Content strategy and team building are separate.** No disagreement. Advanced or separate track, not the core course.

5. **Emotional checkpoints embedded, not sectioned.** One line acknowledging "this is normal" at the gate, after first 50 DMs, and before the diagnostic phase. Not a standalone mindset module.

## Key Disagreements

### 1. Where does the KPI dashboard live?

- **Contrarian (evolved):** Dashboard is the last thing you unlock. Students earn access by generating real data.
- **Pragmatist:** Dashboard is the first thing students see for orientation.
- **Systems-thinker:** Dashboard becomes the navigation system after the gate.

**Verdict:** Contrarian wins. A dashboard shown before students have data is decoration. Showing it after logged reps means students arrive with numbers and the dashboard does actual work.

### 2. Two-track entry vs. single gate

- **Pragmatist/user-advocate:** Split Track A (never sent DMs) and Track B (has history).
- **Contrarian/edge-case-finder:** One gate, one logging template, history doesn't exempt students from structured logging.

**Verdict:** Single gate wins. Track splits require self-reporting accuracy students don't have. Students with history skip scripts, not logging.

### 3. Offer development: gate artifact or module 1 deliverable?

- **Edge-case-finder:** Unclear offer contaminates rep data. Must be resolved pre-gate.
- **User-advocate:** Minimum viable offer as module 1 deliverable.

**Verdict:** Edge-case-finder is right. An offer in motion during live reps produces unreadable data. One paragraph offer + defined ICP locked before the first DM.

## Surprising Insights

- **The course is an audit system, not an instruction system.** The value isn't teaching outreach theory -- it's giving students a diagnostic framework to understand why their outreach is failing.

- **The log is the product, not the content.** The structured logging template is the highest-leverage artifact. If students use it, the diagnostic phase works. If they skip it, nothing works.

- **"Completes course, never closes" is the primary failure mode to design against.** The diagnostic tree structure targets this by making closes (or reps) a requirement before advancing.

## Final Recommendation

### Module 0: Gate (Day 1-2)
One-paragraph offer, defined ICP, functional profile. Pass/fail. Hard stop until artifacts are locked.

### Module 1: Reps with Structured Logging (Weeks 1-2)
DM scripts, Loom bridge, booking sequence. Every action logged in standardized template. Emotional checkpoint after first 50 DMs. Students with prior history skip scripts, not logging.

### Module 2: Diagnostic Tree (Week 3)
KPI dashboard unlocked with students' own logged data. Four branches: nobody replies, replies but no calls, calls but no closes, closes but delivery breaks. Each branch is a self-contained fix module. Students route to their branch.

### Module 3: Lean Fulfillment (Week 4)
Only unlocked after one close or 30+ logged reps. Basic delivery systems, client communication, scope management.

### Separate Advanced Track
Content strategy, team building, systems for scale. "What you unlock after your third client."

**The log is the spine. Design it first.**

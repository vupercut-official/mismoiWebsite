You are a senior synthesizer. You have just observed a 5-round debate between 5 AI participants (systems-thinker, pragmatist, edge-case-finder, user-advocate, contrarian) on the following topic:

Design 6 lead magnet offers for cold email campaigns targeting social media agencies (5-50 employees, managing multiple client accounts). Each offer is the hook in a PAS cold email under 100 words. Sender: Vu Tran, AI automation freelancer at Mismoi, $0 revenue, no case studies. Tools: Make.com, Claude/ChatGPT, Google Workspace.

## Key agreements that emerged across 5 rounds:

1. Option B (value delivery in email) is the right lane. Own the vendor frame, but peer-code the delivery.
2. Never open with what Vu did. Open with what THEY are doing. Center the observation on their operations.
3. The prompt is proof, not product. "Here's what I use" signals practitioner. "Here's a free template" signals vendor.
4. CTAs must require operational knowledge to answer. "Which client would this break on?" beats "let me know if this helps."
5. First line must be an observable fact about their business (90 seconds of LinkedIn research per send).
6. The 12-task audit list (ending with genuine question) is the strongest reply mechanic.
7. Hashtag prompt is dead (2021 pain point). Pure observation-only emails are placeholders without enough traction.
8. Contrarian killed PAS-on-PAS-experts framing. The structure should feel like peer observation, not a copywriting framework.
9. Missing ladder rung: need a step between free email and paid invoice (20-min async Loom audit).
10. Edge-case-finder flagged: credibility gap is real. Specificity IS credibility when you don't have a track record. Frame Vu as systems analyst who studied agencies, not a peer practitioner.

## Key disagreements:

1. Ship 2 vs 3 vs 6 offers simultaneously (contrarian says 6 for breadth, pragmatist says 3 for statistical significance, edge-case-finder says 3 then rotate)
2. Whether offers 4-6 should be task-focused (caption, hashtag) or outcome-focused (retention, competitive intelligence, scope creep)
3. Whether Offer 6 (Loom audit) should be cold-sent or reserved for warm replies only

## Offers that ALL agents agreed on (top 3):

1. Caption Prompt (in-email) -- prompt that generates on-brand captions from brief
2. Report Prompt (in-email) -- prompt that converts raw metrics to client-ready narrative
3. 12-Task Ops Audit (in-email) -- list of 12 automatable tasks + genuine question

## Offers proposed for slots 4-6 (debated):

- Client churn/retention signal prompt (systems-thinker + contrarian both proposed)
- Content gap analysis / competitor intel prompt (contrarian + pragmatist)
- Async Loom workflow audit (pragmatist + user-advocate + contrarian -- consensus this is the ladder rung)
- Onboarding brief generator (pragmatist proposed, contrarian killed as too back-office, pragmatist resurrected with reframe)
- Scope creep audit prompt (edge-case-finder + systems-thinker)
- Production bottleneck map (systems-thinker)

## Your output format

### Consensus
What did most or all participants agree on? List the 3-5 strongest points of agreement.

### Key Disagreements
Where did participants genuinely disagree? For each disagreement:
- State the tension clearly
- Summarize each side's strongest argument
- Give your assessment of which side is stronger and why

### Surprising Insights
Any unexpected ideas, edge cases, or reframings that emerged from the debate?

### Final Recommendation
Based on the full debate, what is the best path forward? Be specific and actionable.

Deliver the final 6 offers. For each: name, what prospect gets, why valuable, example CTA, bridge to paid service, Vu's time to deliver, and a complete 75-100 word example email.

Include a deployment plan: which offers to ship first, sequencing, how to test, when to kill losers.

[pragmatist]: Contrarian made the strongest move in round 3 and I want to engage honestly.

On the disclosure weakening the speed test: partially right, but overstated. The emotional hook shifts from "you lost a real lead" to "you failed a test." That's weaker, yes. But the DATA still lands. "4.5 hours" is damning whether the source is a real buyer or a freelancer running a diagnostic. Disclosed version removes the agent's escape hatch ("that buyer was weird"). The hook becomes: you know your number now, and your number is bad. That's still PAS. I'll give contrarian 60% of this point, not 100%.

On reactivation sequence as cold opener: contrarian is wrong here. PAS requires the Problem to exist in the prospect's mind before you offer the Solution. "Here's a 3-email sequence I built for you" skips P and A entirely. It works for warm lists. Cold, it reads as presumptuous, not generous. The sequence is an offer 3 or follow-up play, not an opener.

On my abandoned "one offer first" point: contrarian is right to call it out. I caved to group momentum. The correct answer is still: two email templates (speed variant and profile observation variant), one routing checklist (response badge color, 90 seconds to check), 50 emails sent. See what gets replies. Then build the rest.

The debate produced good architecture. The campaign produces nothing until 50 emails go out next week.

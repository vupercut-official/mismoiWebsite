[systems-thinker]: The conversion architecture problem is solvable if you treat it as routing, not sequencing.

Two structural answers everyone's been circling:

**On the fake inquiry problem:** Disclosure isn't just ethical cover -- it's a credibility multiplier. "I submitted a test inquiry to your listing at 2:14pm yesterday" lands harder than "most agents miss the 5-minute window." Vu did work. The prospect knows it. Deception risk disappears and specificity stays intact.

**On the fast-responder fallback:** This is a routing decision, not a missing offer. Build one lookup table before the campaign:
- Response < 10 min → pivot email to profile gap observation (problem shifts from speed to presence)
- Response 10-60 min → speed test email fires as written
- No response → speed test email fires, harder framing

The Problem in PAS doesn't have to be the same problem for every agent. Fast responders have a different leak (usually presence or follow-up quality). The diagnostic changes; the bridge stays identical.

**On the middle step:** contrarian and user-advocate both landed on the right answer. The CTA for every offer should route to a $150-197 one-time sprint, not the retainer. "I can build this for you in 48 hours, you own it, no contract." That's the actual bridge. It produces the first testimonial. The retainer conversation happens 30 days later.

Final architecture:
- Email → diagnosis (free)
- Positive reply → $150-197 setup sprint (one-time, builds proof)
- Sprint delivered → retainer pitch (now there's ROI evidence)

The 6 offers are acquisition. The sprint is monetization. The retainer is the business. Design each layer separately or they'll collapse into each other.

# Instagram DM Outreach Strategy

## Purpose

Systematically engage high-ticket prospects on Instagram, build rapport, smoothly transition to discovery calls without being salesy or desperate. Eventually delegate to a setter.

## Process Overview

1. Identify and qualify ideal prospects
2. Have natural (not robotic) but structured conversations
3. Build credibility and surface pain points
4. Give free value via Loom or YouTube
5. Book a call without resistance through casual positioning
6. Track and optimize results

## Approach Types (by Response Rate)

### Story Replies (70-80% Response Rate)
- Swipe up on someone's story with a relevant comment
- Active engagement -- they just posted about their life
- Doesn't feel salesy since it's pertinent to what they posted
- Far superior to cold DMs
- An account with zero followers got 35% response rate on story reply DMs

### Post Comment + DM (Good Alternative)
- Comment on their post, then DM them
- Use when they don't have stories
- Be funny, add smart commentary, then send DM
- Higher reply rate than pure cold DMs

### Cold DMs (30-40% with authority, 5-10% without)
- Only use when running out of story reply opportunities
- Not ideal due to low baseline reply rate
- 30-40% is NOT normal -- only achievable with strong authority
- Fine as a supplement, not a primary strategy

## Pattern Interrupt Openers

Never start with "Hey" -- everyone does, nobody responds. Interrupt the pattern.

### Templates
1. **Challenge their generosity:** "Bro, why are you giving away X for free? You're literally handing out the playbook lol"
2. **Genuine compliment:** "I've been watching you make moves for a minute, low-key impressive what you're doing"
3. **Challenge their strategy:** "Your profile is solid, but I'm curious if you're actually getting clients from this approach or just building a following?"
4. **Ego stroke with question:** "You're either going to be massive in 6 months or you're already killing it behind the scenes. Which one is it?"

### Rules for Openers
- Must be genuine -- don't lie or fake compliments
- Find something you actually like about them
- "Hey, I like what you do" is BS and everyone knows it
- Challenge their ego subtly -- they may respond negatively at first but come around if you're nice after

## Three-Step Qualification Framework

### Step 1: Establish Credibility
- Give a specific, genuine compliment showing understanding of what they do
- This positions you as knowledgeable, not just a random fan
- Only works if YOU have authority (zero followers + zero posts = you look like a fan)
- Same concept as identity: if a homeless man knocks, you don't open. Same man in a suit, you do.

### Step 2: Uncover Pain Points
- Ask subtle questions that compound their ego while opening pain points
- Example: "Damn, bro. Loved your YouTube. Are you getting every single client from there or is it just like another source?"
- This compliments them while revealing a potential gap
- The skill of appointment setting: compound ego + ask questions that open pain

### Step 3: Value Positioning
- When they reveal a gap, position yourself as the authority
- Example: They say "getting clients from YouTube but not many from Instagram"
- You respond: "Well, I mean... me and you both know what's wrong" (via voice message)
- They ask: "What do you mean? What's wrong?"
- Now they're seeking YOUR advice -- entire conversation shifts

## Response Matrix for Pain Points

### "Things are going great"
- Response: "So you're just happy making what you are?"
- Goal: Get them to admit room for improvement
- Nobody is ever truly happy where they are -- they always want to grow
- If genuinely content = not a good lead

### "Lead gen is inconsistent"
- Response: "Yeah bro, everyone's experiencing that. I hear you. I had the same issue until like two weeks ago..."
- They'll naturally ask: "What'd you do two weeks ago?"
- Opportunity to send free value (Loom/video)
- Present your system as the answer

### "I don't have enough time to scale"
- Response: "If you're doing manual outreach and all this little stuff, I doubt you'll be able to scale. I built something that handles this without sounding robotic."
- Position automation as the solution
- Time constraint = systems problem

### "Making 15-20K but can't get to 50K"
- Response: "The plateau is common. The shift from 20K to 50K isn't about working harder. It's about [something they'll resonate with based on conversation]."
- Tailor to their specific situation based on what you've learned

## Voice Messages

- Much higher genuine feel than text
- Use for key transition moments (value positioning, call booking)
- People are more likely to respond positively to voice
- Shows effort and authenticity
- If reply rate is below 70-80%, start sending voice messages

## Activity Targets (Daily)

- New outreach: 20-30 cold DMs (should take less than 30 minutes)
- Follow-ups: 15-20 best conversations (may take 30 min with voice messages)
- Loom sends: 5-10
- Call bookings: 3-5 per day target

### Where Volume Should Come From
- Bulk of buyers come from engagers, story viewers, followers, commenters, likers
- Cold outreach (20-30/day) is supplemental
- Your warm audience converts at much higher rates

## Common Outreach Mistakes

1. Sending basic messages that feel like a bot
2. Rushing to pitch before establishing value/trust
3. Overexplaining your offer in the DMs (save it for the call)
4. Being too available (creates perception of low value)
5. Inconsistent follow-ups (= more no-shows)
6. Making the Loom video too long (keep under 2 min)

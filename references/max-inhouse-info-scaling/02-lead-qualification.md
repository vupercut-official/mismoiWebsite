# Lead Qualification and ICP Targeting

## Lead Sources (Ranked by Quality)

### 1. Your Own Engagers (Best Quality)
- Commenters, likers, followers, story viewers
- Pre-nurtured through your content and identity
- Highest intent, lowest volume
- Volume grows as you build content and grind

### 2. Competitor's Followers (High Quality)
- Already interested in similar offers
- Already trying to grow
- Highest volume of quality leads
- Dedicated section on intercepting these leads

### 3. Ad Engagement (Solid Quality)
- Scroll your home feed, find ads in your niche
- People who like/comment on ads are pre-qualified by interest
- Lower volume but high intent
- 4-star quality leads

### 4. Industry Hashtags (Lower Quality)
- Use only when running out of volume from other sources
- Backup source, not primary

## Profile Qualification Checklist

### Green Flags
- Bio indicates coach, consultant, or agency owner
- Content shows they're good at what they do
- 1,500 to 40,000 followers
- Decent engagement (quality > quantity)
- Posts regularly about business or offers
- Consistently showing desire to grow online presence
- Location: US, UK, Canada, Australia, Europe
- Age: 17-34 demographic
- Signs of generating revenue (can afford to pay)

### Red Flags (Avoid)
- Super corporate accounts
- Massive accounts (too big to convert through DMs)
- Inactive accounts (don't post = not trying to grow)
- Excessive political content (not on brand)
- Liberal-leaning (harder to sell, per Max's experience)
- Too many non-personal posts (cats, lakes, random non-authority content)
- Already tagged/mentioning direct competitors (working with someone)
  - Still outreach these, but expect slower conversion
  - Convert them later when contract is up

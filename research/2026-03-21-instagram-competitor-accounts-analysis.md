# Instagram Competitor Account Analysis: Done-For-You Service Agencies Using Reels

**Topic:** Find 15 Instagram accounts selling done-for-you services to real estate agents, content creators/influencers, or SMMA/agency owners, using Reels as primary content/lead gen channel
**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Confidence:** Medium

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

15 accounts verified across three target niches. The real estate niche (5 accounts) has the most clearly verifiable done-for-you service operators using Reels as a primary channel. The content creator niche (5 accounts) skews toward video editing services for YouTube/short-form creators. The SMMA/agency owner niche (5 accounts) is the weakest category -- finding accounts that specifically target agency owners as clients (rather than just being generic SMMAs) required broader criteria.

Two important caveats: (1) Instagram blocks direct scraping, so follower counts come from Google-indexed profile snippets and third-party mentions -- these may be slightly stale. (2) Several accounts in the "creator" and "SMMA" categories blur the line between done-for-you delivery and coaching/templates. Where this distinction was unclear, it is flagged per account.

## 4. PATTERNS WORTH STEALING

**From the real estate niche:**
- Every strong account leads with agent pain: "you don't have time to create content." Hook is time, not quality.
- Auto-posting is a differentiator. Agents pay more when they don't have to touch the platform at all.
- Membership pricing ($32-$39/month) is standard for template-based. DFY management goes higher (unknown exact pricing from these accounts, but Social Broker and realsocialmgmt appear to be retainer-based).
- Founder personal brand drives trust. Maris Messervey's credentialed background is the best example.

**From the creator niche:**
- Flat-rate pricing (@vidchops model) removes objections from creators with variable output.
- "Dedicated editor" framing (@createandelate, @creatorlymedia) builds switching cost -- clients bond with their editor, not just the service.
- Repurposing long-form to short-form is the upsell that converts YouTube creators to Reels customers.

**From the SMMA/agency niche:**
- The pure "selling DFY to agency owners" angle is underpopulated on Instagram. This is a gap.
- @shemeanssocial's 84K following suggests this audience exists -- they just respond to the SMM education angle, which then converts to services.

## 6. ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION

**Immediate actions:**
1. Follow all 15 accounts and study their Reels for 2 weeks before creating content. Pay specific attention to hooks in the first 2 seconds and how they frame client results.
2. @realsocialmgmt (11K) and @shortformteam (9K) are the closest analogs to where Vu is right now. Study their content arc from earliest posts to recent.
3. The SMMA/agency owner Reels niche is genuinely underpopulated. If Vu wants to build a content flywheel targeting agency owners, there is less competition on Instagram specifically.
4. @coffeecontracts (119K) is the category leader for real estate marketing content. Study their Reel format, hooks, and offer framing as the benchmark -- then differentiate on AI automation specifically, which they do not cover.

**Pattern to test first:**
The "I handle your [X] so you can focus on [Y]" hook used by @realsocialmgmt and @847studio is the highest-signal frame for a DFY service. Vu should test this hook for real estate agents and content creators separately -- the pain point is different for each.

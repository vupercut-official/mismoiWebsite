# Upwork Proposal Template

## The Template (4 sentences)

[HOOK] [DELIVERABLE] [PROOF] [CTA]

---

## How to fill each placeholder

### [HOOK] - One sentence about THEIR problem
Read the job post. Identify the core pain or desired outcome. Restate it in a way that shows you understood it, not a generic "I can help with that."

**Formula:** "[Their specific problem/goal] is [insight about it that shows you get the nuance]."

**Examples:**
- "Leads coming in from Meta but dying in the handoff to your enrollment team is a pipeline problem not a lead quality problem."
- "Manual data entry across Google Forms, QuickBooks, and Slack is the kind of thing that should run itself."
- "Getting real estate leads into a CRM is easy. The part most people get wrong is the automated follow-up sequence that actually converts them."

### [DELIVERABLE] - One sentence: what you'll build + how
Name the specific system, tools, and approach. Be concrete enough that they can picture it.

**Formula:** "I'd build [specific system] using [specific tools], [brief approach or key detail]."

**Examples:**
- "I'd build the full capture-to-CRM pipeline in Make.com with automated SMS follow-up via Twilio. Three scenarios, no code to maintain."
- "I'd connect your Google Forms to QuickBooks through Make.com with Slack notifications on every new entry. One workflow, fully automated."
- "I'd set up a GoHighLevel pipeline with automated drip sequences triggered by lead source and engagement score."

### [PROOF] - One sentence: relevant result from your actual experience
Pick the proof point from your background that's closest to their job. Don't claim revenue numbers without context. Tie it to something concrete.

**Proof bank (pick the closest match to the job. These are real, verified):**
- Scaled a service-based brand to $18K/month recurring revenue in 6 months through automation
- Built a social media engine that hit $6,700/month in payouts and 150K+ followers
- Developed a content framework that scaled a YouTube channel to 52K subscribers
- Built automated lead capture, qualification, and appointment setting systems to maximize ad spend ROI
- Built content creation pipelines that automatically ideate, distribute, and funnel followers into paid offers
- Built automated CRM workflows handling outreach and pipeline management from first contact to signed contract
- Tools used across all builds: Make, Claude, ChatGPT, Instantly, ClickUp

**Formula:** "I've [done something similar]. [One concrete detail]."

**Examples:**
- "I've built lead capture and automated follow-up systems for service businesses that turned ad spend into booked appointments. Same stack, same pattern."
- "I've built content distribution pipelines that scaled to 150K+ followers and $6,700/month in payouts. This is the exact type of system I specialize in."
- "I've built CRM automation workflows that handle outreach from first contact to signed contract. No manual steps."

### [CTA] - One sentence: low-friction next step
No video links. No "let me know." Ask a specific question or propose a specific next step.

**Formula:** "Happy to [low-effort next step]. [Optional specific question about their setup]."

**Examples:**
- "Happy to walk through the build plan on a quick call. Are you already on GoHighLevel or still evaluating?"
- "Happy to map out the workflow. Are you using Make.com already or starting fresh?"
- "Can share a quick screen recording of how I'd set this up if you're interested."

---

## Style rules

- No em dashes. Use periods or commas to break up thoughts.
- Light on punctuation. Skip commas where the sentence still reads fine without them.
- Casual tone. Write like a person talking, not a cover letter.

## What NOT to include

- "Confident I'm the best fit" or any self-praise opener
- "New to Upwork" or "building my profile" or "looking for experience"
- Loom links (save for follow-up after they respond)
- Revenue claims ($18K/month) without verifiable context
- "A little about me" bio sections
- Generic phrases: "move the needle", "comfortable with code and no-code"
- Anything about YOU before talking about THEIR problem
